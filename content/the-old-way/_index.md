Most of what you see online is chosen for you. Not by editors, not by your friends, not by the people who made it — by systems designed to keep you looking at the screen for as long as possible. That is not a conspiracy. It is a business model, and it shapes everything. {{< cite "doctorow-enshittification-2023" >}}

This section explains, in plain terms, how that model works and what it costs — not in money, but in the quality of what gets made and the attention you spend on it. It is grounded in research, honest about what the evidence does and does not show, and written for people who would rather understand a system than be told how to feel about it.
