## Design Context

### Users
General public readers. Mixed contexts across phone and desktop, daytime and evening. The site should work well for quick visits as well as longer reading sessions.

### Brand Personality
Editorial, calm, assured. Warm and direct without being preachy. British English. The reader should leave feeling more capable, not judged.

### Aesthetic Direction
Single theme: an ink-like dark theme that avoids “pitch black”. Low-glare surfaces with generous typography, clear hierarchy, and restrained accents drawn from the section wayfinding colours.

### Design Principles
- Optimise for reading: comfortable contrast, line length, and rhythm on text-heavy pages.
- Make navigation feel obvious: clear signposts, previews, and in-page structure (TOCs) where content is long.
- Keep the system simple: small set of tokens and components; avoid one-off styling.
- Progressive enhancement: everything works without JS; JS only enhances.
- Wayfinding over decoration: section colours guide, they do not dominate.
